Exploring the Catholic Social Teachings

Hover your mouse over any of the topics to learn about them.

Human Dignity

This is the core of the Catholic Social Teachings. Everything we do and all the actions we take should be focused on the human dignity. The idea is simple, treat people as you wish to be treated.

Workers Rights and Dignity of Work

Work is about more than making money, it is an active contribution to society and Gods creation. If human dignity is to be protected, the dignity of the worker is included. Fair wages, working hours, and vacation time are all important to the workers dignity. Additionally the nature of work is to be considered.
"The economy must serve the people, not the other way around" - US Conference of Catholic Bishops

Family and Community Participation

Your Family and Community are at the core of human dignity. You immidiate and connected social circles are paramount to your ability to grow as a person. The support you do or don't recieve and give determines if you and others have the extra help they need, when they need it.

Rights and Responsibilities

Everyone is born with a set of universal rights including life, liberty, and the pursuit of happiness. These rights preserve the dignity of the person. Additionally, the rights come with responsibilities to support our families, eachother, and the larger society.

Protection for the Less Fortunate

Even if you find yourself well off, consider that you to could be in this position. The poor and vaulerable are the ones who need the most help, but often the ones who get the least. Jesus commands his disciples, and in turn us, to put the poor first and care for those who need it most.

Care for the Earth

Every day is earthday. Showing respect and care for the animals and environment around us is showing respect to God and his work. We are conservation minded and environmentally concious creatures with the power to take care of our planet.

Solidarity

Solidarity is an agreement between and support for the members of a group towards a cause. Wether the cause is disaster relief, food scarcity, pollution, and more, we show love by commiting our efforts to solving these problems. At the core of solidarity is the pursuit of peace and justice for all. Love the needs of others and fufill them as best you can.
